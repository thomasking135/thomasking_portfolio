About Me

Kia ora, I’m Thomas King, currently studying Interior Décor at the Open Polytechnic while also developing my skills in animation and graphic design. While I enjoy the creative crossover between visual storytelling and spatial design, my long-term vision is to move fully into kitchen design — a space where creativity meets everyday living.

I’m especially passionate about small kitchen design. As more people move into townhouses, apartments, and compact homes, flexible and efficient kitchens are becoming essential. People don’t want to compromise on key appliances, generous prep space, or even the dream of a functional island that allows both cooking and entertaining. With renovation costs rising and many of my friends entering the property market with mortgages and student loans, I understand the importance of designing kitchens that are smart, practical, and financially realistic.

Small kitchens also appeal to me because of accessibility. Having a mother and close friends living with disabilities, I see the importance of thoughtful layouts, accessible drawers, and solutions that work around different abilities — especially when a large kitchen simply isn’t an option.

I love kitchens that reflect Aotearoa’s connection to nature — biophilic, sustainably conscious, and eco-friendly spaces that still bring friends and whānau together. Small doesn’t mean boring. My favourite colour is green, my favourite food is my mum’s Greek salad, and heaven for me is a Sunday drive exploring new homes and kitchens around Ōtautahi and across the motu. If I could revolutionise one element, it would be the multifunctional island — a mobile, adaptable station designed for more than just cooking.
